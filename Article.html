4d Gaming .

Written by Frankwise January 12 2009
Entertaiment

Bosch's work on Miegakure has sparked a whole host of new questions about our own reality in the artist's head. "When making this game I have to take each concept and strip away the part that does not generalize to higher dimensions," he tells The Creators Project. If a square is a slice of a cube, then a cube is a slice of a 4D object like the ones Bosch is building for Miegakure. The process of extrapolating each shape up one dimension is what he means by "generalizing to higher dimensions." He continues, "By doing this you get rid of things that are specific to 3D, and you reveal the much more fundamental

core idea." Essentially, Miegakure is a mathematical proof that you can run around in and explore. Maybe this model reflects an unknown dimension of our own world, or maybe it's as made up as Star Wars' hyperdrive. In the new video, Bosch says, "While you could ignore all this while playing the game, to me it feels even more beautiful when you know more about what is happening." We agree.
